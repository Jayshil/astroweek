About

AstroWeek

AstroWeek is a week long workshop on various topics of astrophysics originally organized by the Astronomy club of Sardar Vallabhbhai National Institute of Technology, Surat-7, Gujarat, India. Although the founding members of this club are no longer affiliated to the institute, they keep the tradition of organizing this workshop.


In times of corona, we are organizing this workshop online on Zoom. This time we shall focus on various topics ranging from basics of astronomy to cosmology. The 2021 edition of AstroWeek is not only consist of undergraduate level lectures, but it will also contains the hands-on sessions to work on the real data gathered by various telescopes.

Team

Our Contributors

The speakers for this edition of AstroWeek are mainly Masters and PhD students --- they work on various fields of astrophysics including planetary science, stars and galaxies and cosmology. Daxal is a Master student at University of Padua in Italy where he works on compact stellar objects. Jayshil works on characterising exoplanets as a PhD student in Stockholm University in Sweden. Maulik and Vilasini are research assistants at the University of Geneva in Switzerland where they study structure formation and early universe, respectively.

Topics to be covered

On the first day of AstroWeek, we shall talk about recent theoretical and observational advances done in the field of astronomy.
In addition to this we shall also introduce some of the important computational tools, like python, to study the astronomy.


After getting acquainted with basics of the filed, on the second day, Jayshil will take you on the tour of exoplanets --- the planets
outside the solar system. Here you shall learn about detecting exoplanets (using real observational data), their statistics (again, with real data)
and, also about exoplanetary atmospheres. Finally, you shall learn about prospective of life elsewhere in the universe and current research themes in the field.
